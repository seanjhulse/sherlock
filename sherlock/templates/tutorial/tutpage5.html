SherLOCK - Network Traffic


Our node map is capable of visually displaying traffic represented by an alteration the color of the edge as packets are being sent and received from a node.
It would be more interesting if we could inspect what information is being passed by the packets.

That's what the "Watch Network Traffic" feature accomplishes. This feature grants the user the ability to inspect the packet header of packets sent over an http connection.
Due to the encrypted nature of packets being sent over an https connection, we do not provide the ability to display the contents of the https packet header as the output would be unintelligible encrypted junk.


To access the Network Traffic Console, right-click on the node you would like to inspect as seen earlier in the tutorial and move your cursor to the bottom-right of the context menu and releasing displays a console in the bottom-righthand corner of the screen as shown in the below image:
